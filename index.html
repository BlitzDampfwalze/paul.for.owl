Transparency & Accountability

Why I am running...

Improve electronic record keeping for both effective financial planning and accountability. Without thorough record keeping, it is impossible to be financially prudent.

This means keeping records of work orders, which should be available to all owners as it is their money.

Financial plans and analysis should be thorough and available to owners. The board should seek input from the owners as well.

Involve the community - this could include various community committees to help with research and analysis for better long-term decisions.

As a board member, I will work to improve communication and transparency as well as act as an advocate for the owners. I also possess in-depth financial knowledge and skills to provide input regarding financial decisions. As an actual resident, I hope to be a responsive voice to improve The Oaks of Woodlake community as a whole.

Ideas:
  • Use technology to improve record keeping of work orders, status, actions, and improving long-term financial planning
  • Improved financial planning means getting the most for our dollar not only today, but for years to come
  • Relay updates quickly regarding the decision making process of the board's actions - particularly as it pertains to individual owners
  • Provide oversight and accountability via transparent documents that already belong to the owners
  • Address legal matters and update laws to hold the board accountable including inaction by the board
  • Provide a plan of action to owners where it is the association's responsibility
